OUR HISTORY

ROYAL MAGGIANO's is an Indian fast food company, founded in 1940 as a restaurant operated by Chaurasiya,. They rechristened their business as a hamburger stand, and later turned the company into a franchise, with the Golden Arches logo being introduced in 1953 at a location in Phoenix, Arizona.
In 1955, Ray korc, a businessman, joined the company as a franchise agent and proceeded to purchase the chain from the LNS Group. ROYAL MAGGIANO's had its previous headquarters in Oak Brook, Mumbai, but moved its global headquarters to Chicago in June 2018. ROYAL MAGGIANO's is the world's largest restaurant chain by revenue, serving over 69 million customers daily in over 100 countries across 37,855 outlets as of 2018. Although ROYAL MAGGIANO's is best known for its hamburgers, cheeseburgers and french fries, they feature products, breakfast items, soft drinks, milkshakes, and desserts.
In response to changing consumer tastes and a negative backlash because of the unhealthiness of their food, the company has added to its menu healthy food. The LNS GROUP revenues come from the rent, royalties, and fees paid by the franchisees, as well as sales in company-operated restaurants.
